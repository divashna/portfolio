COVID Connect

The importance of public health messaging

Context

The outbreak of COVID-19 and the spread of misinformation that followed highlighted the need to keep ethnic and minority communities in Australia engaged with public health messages regarding COVID-19 symptoms and testing.

My Role

  • Product Designer
  • Researcher

    • Status  COMPLETE

    The COVID Connect case study is now complete. This valuable learning experience enabled me to develop my research, UX and communication skills while conducting research and testing with diverse user groups.

    Understanding the Market

    In order to understand the issues multicultural communities face when accessing health information, I first conducted exploratory research on the barriers and pain points experienced by such communities. As part of my research, I conducted:

    • Secondary background research
    • A SWOT analysis of four competitor services: the COVIDSafe app, Coronavirus Australia app, Coronavirus (COVID-19) Health Alert and the Australian Government WhatsApp Channel for COVID-19
    • An online ethnography of 62 reviews from the four competitors; and
    • A questionnaire which produced 19 unique insights from users

    The results were in, and the problems were clear. My research revealed that multicultural communities had previously been disadvantaged by the public health system, which exacerbated their access to accurate and reliable information. These issues were fuelled by:

    • A lack of engagement with community and/or cultural leaders
    • Poor accessibility
    • Limited availability of resources
    • Poor multilingual support; and
    • Limited use of visual media, such as videos, icons and infographics

    Defining the Target Audience

    The research indicated that a major weakness of existing health messages was a lack of multilingual support, which limited engagement with emerging health advice. This pain point was consequently selected as the focus of the product. A problem statement was established to simplify the problem and highlight key user needs that would need to be addressed in the final design:

    Multicultural communities cannot engage with COVID-19 public health messages as they arrive due to a lack of suppport provided for multilingual and non-English speakers.

    How might we develop a platform where individuals can instantaneously and actively engage with health messages in order to reduce misconceptions regarding coronavirus symptoms and testing procedures?

    Visualising the User

    A persona was created to highlight the issues encountered by individuals belonging to minority and ethnic communities in relation to public health messages about COVID-19.

    Exploring Ideas

    The next step was to explore solutions through sketches and interactive wireframes.

    Validating the Designs

    Usability testing sessions were conducted with multiple users to determine the feasibility and usability of the application. The tests revealed that the solution was viable and accessible to multicultural communities and users expressed their satisfaction of the product in allowing minority and ethnic groups to engage with public health messages regarding COVID-19 symptoms and testing.

    The findings from the usability tests were summarised into affinity diagrams and relevant changes to the design and layout of the COVID Connect platform were implemented.

    A high-fidelity prototype of the final concept, COVID Connect, was then created. The website was designed to automatically offer multilingual support through one's browser, whereas information would also be made accessible through the use of icons and images.

    Key features include:

    • Contacting local community leaders in one's language of choice
    • Reliable information from trusted sources
    • Accessibility through videos, icons, flags, infographics and text
